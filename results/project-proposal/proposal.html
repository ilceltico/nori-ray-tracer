**Final Project Proposal** Student name: Federico Stella Sciper number: 342796 Scene description and motivation ================================ I interpret the theme "Change" in two opposite ways. On one side (darker), time inevitably passes and all things change, and I feel this through melancholy. On the other side (a bit brighter), it's only through change that something new can begin. These very simple ideas led me to the rough sketch above, which is intended to be primarily viewed from left to right, as suggested by the god rays coming through the window. On the left, we have an old house, with old but well maintained furniture, and a table with 2 glasses filled with some liquid (yet to decide), signifying that the house is being maintained by two elderly people, grandparents. The more you move to the right, the more time has passed, and it's possible to notice a single pair of slippers, signifying that one of the two elderly people has finished their path in this world. More to the right, the furniture and floor look worse, with some ants on the ground, as the second person joins the first in another world. Finally, on the far right, boxes are portrayed, signifying a relocation. This hopefully creates a sense of sadness in the viewer, especially through the yellow god rays coming through the left, with an overall look inspired by Caravaggio's "Vocazione di san Matteo": The passing of time is further signified by the clocks, which are oddly many. The clock on the left reads 6h, and the yellow light from the window suggests a sunset. The pendulum in the center reads 8h, the one on the floor reads 10h, and finally the one on the right reads 12h, midnight. However, the same scene can be re-read in a different key, and this is suggested by the painting on the right, with a sunny scene and kids playing. This painting will emit some light, in order to seem like it could either be a painting or a window. In this new light (quite literally), instead of moving out the old stuff in sadness, the right-most clock can signify noon, the peak of the day and of life, with the new family (children or grandchildren of the previous owners) moving in. The clock on the right further signifies this by being in a very modern style, as opposed to all the others. The clocks and ants are citations from Salvador Dali, in particular "The Persistence of Memory": Moreover, environment lighting will be used to decrease the noise and contrast in the scene, and I will use a modern house for this lighting effect. As a consequence, modern details will be reflected through the glasses and liquids (and maybe a mirror) from the left side of the scene, showing an odd interaction that defies time, and that goes from the time of the right (which could be present or future) to the time of the elderly on the left (which could be past or present), and signifies the joy of the grandparents in seeing or imagining the lives of their children and grandchildren.
